Mechanical vs. Chemical Digestion

Mechanical digestion is the breakdown of food into smaller pieces without changing the actual molecules. Chemical digestion is where the food is broken down by enzymes into small, soluble enzymes so that the nutrients can be absorbed.
An example of mechanical digestion is when the mouth chews food. The food molecules don't actually change, but the food is broken down into smaller pieces.
An example of chemical digestion is when food is broken down in the stomach by stomach acid. The food is now almost fully broken down, and can be passed to the small and large intestines to have its nutrients absorbed.
